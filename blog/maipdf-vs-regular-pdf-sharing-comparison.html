The Problem with Regular PDF Sharing

When you share a PDF through email, cloud storage, or messaging apps, that document can be:

  • Downloaded and saved permanently
  • Forwarded to unauthorized parties
  • Stored in email archives indefinitely
  • Accessed years later from old emails
  • Compromised if accounts are hacked

MaiPDF self-destructing PDFs solve these problems by automatically deleting documents after they've been viewed a set number of times, ensuring sensitive information doesn't persist indefinitely.

PDF Security Statistics

According to recent studies, 68% of data breaches involve documents that were shared but never properly secured or deleted. Regular PDF sharing creates permanent records that can be accessed months or years later, while MaiPDF ensures documents are automatically deleted after use.

Security Comparison

Regular PDF Sharing Security Risks

  • Permanent Storage – PDFs stay in email inboxes, cloud storage, and chat history forever
  • Unlimited Access – Documents can be opened unlimited times
  • Easy Forwarding – PDFs can be easily forwarded to unauthorized parties
  • Download and Save – Recipients can download and save PDFs permanently
  • Account Hacking Risk – If email or cloud accounts are hacked, PDFs are exposed
  • No Access Control – Can't limit how many times documents are viewed

MaiPDF Security Advantages

  • Automatic Deletion – PDFs are permanently deleted after open count limit is reached
  • Open Count Limits – Control exactly how many times documents can be accessed
  • Time-Based Expiration – Reading sessions expire after set duration
  • Access Tracking – Email notifications show when PDFs are opened
  • No Permanent Storage – Documents aren't stored indefinitely
  • Link Invalidation – Links become useless after limit is reached
