What is MaiPDF?

MaiPDF is a specialized service that allows you to create self-destructing PDFs with advanced security features. Unlike regular PDF sharing where documents can be downloaded, forwarded, and accessed indefinitely, MaiPDF enables you to share PDFs that automatically delete after being viewed a set number of times or after a specific time period.

With MaiPDF, you can set open count limits to control exactly how many times your PDF can be opened before it's automatically deleted. This makes it perfect for sharing confidential documents, contracts, reports, or any sensitive information that shouldn't be accessible indefinitely.

Why Self-Destructing PDFs Matter

According to recent studies, 68% of data breaches involve documents that were shared but never properly secured or deleted. MaiPDF solves this problem by ensuring PDFs automatically delete after reaching their open count limit, reducing the risk of unauthorized access to sensitive documents.

How MaiPDF Self-Destructing PDFs Work

Step 1: Upload Your PDF

Visit MaiPDF.com and upload your PDF document. The service supports standard PDF files and processes them securely.

Step 2: Set Open Count Limit

This is the key feature of MaiPDF. You can set exactly how many times your PDF can be opened:

  • 1 time – PDF deletes after being opened once (true self-destructing)
  • 2-5 times – Allow multiple authorized viewers before deletion
  • Custom limit – Set any number up to 99,999,999 opens

Once the open count limit is reached, the PDF is permanently deleted and the link becomes invalid.

Step 3: Set Reading Session Duration

In addition to open count limits, you can set how long each viewing session lasts (in seconds). This ensures that even if someone opens the PDF, they have a limited time to view it before it expires.

Step 4: Generate Secure Link

MaiPDF generates a unique, secure link to your PDF. This link can only be used up to the open count limit you've set.

Step 5: Share and Monitor

Share the link with your intended recipients. You can optionally enable email notifications to know when the PDF is opened. Once the open count limit is reached, the PDF is automatically deleted.

Understanding Open Count Limits

The open count limit is the maximum number of times your PDF can be opened before it's automatically deleted. Here's how it works:

  • Open Count = 1 – True self-destructing PDF. Deletes immediately after first view.
  • Open Count = 3 – PDF can be opened 3 times total, then automatically deletes.
  • Open Count = 10 – Allows 10 separate opens before deletion.

Important Notes:

  • Each time someone clicks the link and the PDF loads, it counts as one open
  • Refreshing the page counts as another open
  • Once the limit is reached, the PDF is permanently deleted
  • The link becomes invalid and cannot be used again
